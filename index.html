About Us

Malayali Association of Delaware Valley, popularly know as Kalaa, was officially formed as a community organization in October 1979. KALAA is an acronym for Kerala Art and Literary Association of America.To bring together the Malayalees in the Delaware Valley and work for the social, cultural and educational advancement of its members and the community at large was the goal of the founding members of the group.

The beginning of Kalaa was the outcome of long discussions and negotiations. People worked together in presenting the drama taunted the idea of starting a Malayalee association in Philadelphia.

After many meetings and prolonged discussion, the organization formed in October 1979.Today Kalaa is one of the largest Malayalee Associations in North America with more than four hundred members.True to its original spirit, Kalaa has made extraordinary services to the Malayalee community and the Indian community in general. Numerous cultural, literary and educational programs were coordinated by Kalaa. It also annually celebrated the major festivals of Kerala Conducting Malayalam schools, presenting Malayalam movies and presenting guest artists from Kerala are also in Kalaa’s activity list.

Vision and Mission

Vision
Kalaa is a Not for profit [501(C) 3] organization formed in 1979 and incorporated in the Commonwealth of Pennsylva- nia. It operates under a constitution and By-laws approved by the General Body. Officers of the organization are elected annually according to its Bylaws.
Mission
  • To conduct and coordinate charitable, educational, literary, scientific, cultural and social activities for the Indian community of Kerala origin in the Delaware Valley and to help the poor and the needy here in America and abroad
  • To create and promote a better understanding and friendlier relationship among the members of the above community and to develop appreciation, understanding, and love of the art and literature of India
  • To bring about closer understanding between the Kerala immigrant community and the American community at large
  • To engage in other activities incidental or connected with the foregoing objectives or in advancement thereof as permitted under the regulations for Not-For-Profit corporations
  • To impart political and civic education and awareness among the members of the association.
